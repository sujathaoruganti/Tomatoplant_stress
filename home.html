Water Stress Levels

Level 1

Healthy

Control Plants

A healthy tomato plant exhibits vibrant, dark green, softly-fuzzed leaves without spots or holes. Its stems are strong and solid, and the plant overall appears vigorous and well-developed, with no signs of wilting or yellowing.

Level 2

Mild Stress

Plants slightly wilted

Mild water stress in tomato plants can cause leaf wilting and drooping, especially during the hottest part of the day. Leaves may also start to curl or roll, and growth may be slightly stunted.

Level 3

Moderate Stress

Leaf discoloration starts

Moderate stress in tomato plants can manifest in several ways, including wilting, yellowing or discoloration of leaves, stunted growth, and even blossom-end rot. These symptoms often indicate issues with water uptake, nutrient availability, or environmental conditions like heat or drought.

Level 4

Severe Stress

Tomato plants wilted and yield affected

Severe stress in tomato plants can manifest in various symptoms, including leaf curling, wilting, stunted growth, and reduced fruit production. These symptoms can be caused by a range of factors, such as heat stress, water stress, nutrient deficiencies, or diseases.
